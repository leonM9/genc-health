Background & Statement of the Problem

Centralized records, repeated breaches.

The integrity of Philippine healthcare depends on seamless data exchange — but the country still relies on centralized legacy systems that are vulnerable to cyber-attacks, prone to internal corruption, and inefficient in interoperability.

Philhealth Breach

Millions of patient records exposed in 2023. Demonstrates centralized-DB fragility.

PSA Ghost Claims

Death certificates not linked to insurance → fraudulent payouts for deceased patients.

Manual Paper Flow

Patients carry paper records between hospitals — no interoperability, easy tampering.

"How can hospitals deploy a scalable, privacy-conscious, decentralized identity system to authenticate patients and records?"

Chapter 1 · Introduction
03 / 13
Objectives of the Study

One general goal. Five specific deliverables.

General Objective

Design and develop a decentralized web application using AES-256, CP-ABE, and LPA to provide a scalable, cost-effective, privacy-preserving mechanism for verifying hospital records under a hybrid blockchain architecture.

  1. Build three integrated modules: Registry Portal, Patient Wallet, and Provider Portal.
  2. Implement privacy-preserving verification via Zero-Knowledge Proofs.
  3. Design and implement the Layered Proof Aggregation (LPA) protocol — target ≥280 TPS, <$0.01 per record.
  4. Integrate AES-256 + CP-ABE for off-chain payload security with granular access control.
  5. Evaluate software quality using ISO/IEC 25010 standards via UAT with hospital staff.

Chapter 6 · Conclusion
11 / 13
Key Findings

What the evidence supports.

  1. Gen C successfully integrates blockchain with AES-256 + CP-ABE to produce a highly secure environment that addresses the vulnerabilities of centralized medical-record management.
  2. The dual-chain architecture effectively balances data privacy with verifiability — records remain accessible to authorised parties while being mathematically protected from everyone else.
  3. The interface is intuitive and accessible to non-technical users — UAT participants without IT background completed key flows without assistance, confirming the system's robustness and clinical viability.
